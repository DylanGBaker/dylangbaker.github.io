Analysis of my Websites UX and UI Design

The UI and UX concepts I used for my website were found in a lecture that was given by Peta Goldberg. User Experience (UX) is what allows a person to perform actions on an interface. User Interface (UI) is what gives the interface an aesthetic and creates an emotional connection with the user. [1]

Why is this important? The reason we make a website is for a user to traverse through it and experience the content that is on the website. Without UX and UI people probably will not interact with your website. As mentioned, the UI creates an emotional connection with the user. If the UI is designed poorly the user may establish a bad emotional connection with the website and hence, they probably will not come back to the website. As a designer and developer, you want people to come back and possibly even share it with people that they know. So UX and UI design is important. [1]

What could be better or is a bad idea?

I’m going to start with what could be better or what was a bad design choice. I prefer to end on a good note.

Design Section:

In the design section I embedded a pdf for the style guide and wireframes. I had seen this used on other websites, but it was probably also because I was lazy to do the CSS and HTML for this section. So, I just assumed that it was a standard thing to do on a website. It also made sense to me to have that stuff in a pdf. However, from feedback and looking at it again it does work but it seems quite disconnected from my websites experience. My website uses dark colours. The embedded pdf is white and the page numbers at the top have a different font. This does feel weird and sort of creates a disconnect from what I have put on the page and the style that I am going for. It creates a negative emotional connection which is what I don’t want. I will reimplement this using HTML and CSS.

Responsiveness:

About me section:

Due to time constraints the about me section is not very interesting. It needs more work and more styling just so that it does not feel as if it is completely different to everything else and therefore creating a negative emotional response. The same goes for the contact me section.

General Stuff:

I don’t have a footer or header and the text of blog posts for example are quite close to the top and the bottom which makes navigating and reading the blog posts difficult near the top and the bottom.

The blog posts make up a large chunk of the content on the website and so people will spend a large amount of time there. I would like to make this area the most comfortable because of that.

The fonts I chose were based off the lectures which shows that in general serif fonts are good to use for readability [1]. However, the font that I chose although it is serif, could probably be better in terms of readability. It does work but I do think I could choose a different style of serif font for the fonts that are not headings. The headings are using san serif. For now, it is working but it has been suggested to try and make them both serifs. I am using iterative design to make my website so I will try with both using serif and vice versa to see which one people prefer.

The blog section opens a new tab when you click on a blog post. Although this is the way that I prefer to view things on the web it is not the standard. If it’s the same site, then it’s better to just redirect the user to the blog post rather than opening it up in a new tab.

Feedback is also important with user interfaces. I would like to add some feedback on tabs like when someone is on blog post 1 then that button or link is highlighted.

What is working?

I’ll start with the home page. I tried to make use of a law called the Hick’s Law and I used a call to action (CTA). Hick’s Law states that, “The time it takes to make a decision increases with the number of complexity choices [1].” I implemented this with the different sections that you can go to on my website. I did not add more than what I needed and so people don’t feel overwhelmed with the choices they have.

The CTA I used to try and interest a user in finding out more about me. I would like to add more for the blog posts and the portfolio pieces.

The home page also made use of the Law of Locality [1]. These are as follows:

• Law 1: “Put an element where it will effect change [1].”

• Law 2: “If an element effects change across the entire area, put it above the area [1].”

• Law 3: “If an element is far from where it effects change, make it pop [1].”

I used these laws for the redirect links in the nav bar. They affect the entire area of the website when clicked on and so put it right at the top of the website. I was originally going to put these links along the left side of the page but with the number of links and with a 1080p screen it seemed underwhelming and empty and so spreading them across the top made the nav bar feel fuller and appeared to be better due to the law of locality [1].

The blog section shows a snippet of the blog. The idea behind this was by using the Zeigarnik Effect. The Zeigarnik Effect is as follows, “People remember uncompleted or interrupted tasks better than completed tasks [1].” I was trying to pique interest in the user to read the blogs by only giving some of the blog post.

I wanted my site to be friendly and not formal or business like. To give that feeling I chose a background picture that is formal. All the text or buttons with a coloured background are rounded. This creates a more friendly visual to the site which is what I wanted. [1]
