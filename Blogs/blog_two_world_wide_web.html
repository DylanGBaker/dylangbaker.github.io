World-Wide Web

The authors of this text go into depth about what makes up the World-Wide Web (W3). We have covered the basics of what a URL is or what the HTTP is. I will not go into what those do but how those connect people from opposite sides of the world and encourage collaboration and learning.

In the opening paragraph it mentions that the W3 has succeeded in becoming a large library of knowledge however, it has surpassed that and has now become a “sea of knowledge” [1]. The ocean covers a large amount of the earth, and we don’t even know everything about it. Majority of it has not even been explored so calling it an ocean of knowledge is fair as we cannot possibly read and obtain every bit of knowledge that is on the internet. This just shows how big the internet had become in 1994. Trying to imagine how big it has gotten in the next 28 years is somewhat impossible. What is amazing is the fact that we have access to all this knowledge just from a computer and a browser. [1]

We are sharing and expanding on the knowledge as well as collaborating by writing these critical analyses. A more relatable example of collaborating through the internet would be with GitHub. Assume we were in a group making a website. GitHub allows us to upload code to a remote repository so that we would be able to collaborate without having to use a USB stick with all the scripts for example. With a USB stick you would have to wait for the other person to finish editing a script before you can continue with it. GitHub allows us to collaborate at the same time. This increases efficiency and makes sharing code much easier.

An example which I find interesting is with supercomputers and quantum computers. Researchers from around the world can gain access to a computer with high processing power from the other side of the world. Allowing people to connect to it through the internet they have encouraged collaboration and could add to the sea of knowledge for other people to then collaborate on.

Quantum computers are still far away from being in a home for someone to use but people are now able to write code for a quantum computer and play games on one from across the world. I will leave these two links in the citations below if anyone is interested in looking at it.

It truly is amazing how far the W3 has come. We can collaborate and add to a sea of knowledge all because of a URL, HTTP, HTML, and servers. [1] This is the reason the entire world is connected. Just imagine where we would be without the internet. It would be difficult to collaborate and help each other right now without it.
